My Favorite Synths And Voicebanks

There are hundreds of Vocal Synths and Voicebanks out there, but a few are particularly fun in my opinion, and this is my website so you get to listen to me talk about them if you're actually reading this.



  Kasane Teto is my favorite vocal synth of all time, for many, many reasons. Her voice is low enough that I can actually undertand it without memorizing most of the lyrics, which among japanese voicebanks like her is honestly rare. (Hatsune Miku why must you scream in my ear...) The songs she's most well known for are very well written - you can find two of them right below this textbox - She actually has some decent english songs, her design is amazing.

  Fun fact, her design was made as a response to how popular Hatsune Miku was on popular video sharing platform Nico Nico Douga. She was made as an April Fools prank on 2chan, and everything about her, from her haircolor to her likes were decided by rolling a die and then taking the number and having that comment become official.

  Her first name is actually derived from the song Tetopettinson, her gender is Chimera, which is an actual form of intersexuality, and she is 31 years old with specifically bloody red eyes. Oh, and because she was made in 2008, she is great at getting extensions on DVD rentals. She is, in her entirety, a joke.

  Which is, of course, why the internet immediately loved her. She was given a free voicebank on Utau, one of the first three 'Utauloids', and was the most popular Utau by far for well over a decade. Of course, being stuck on Utau still made her relatively niche, until 2023, anyways.

  Then, it was announced she was getting an official Synthesizer V voicebank and redesign. Now? She is the face of modern vocal synthesizing, rivaling Hatsune Miku in popularity. (For context, Hatsune Miku is as popular in Japan as Katy Perry is in America)

  • Watchacallitsname
    A song about trying to find an excuse to reconnect
  • Machine Love
    A song about artifice, and a computer falling in love

Adachi Rei

  After the successful creation of a real life Hatsune Miku robot, Missile39 decided he wanted to expand further by building a new, better made robot girl. Through crowdfunding he received 3.5 million yen, and thus, Adach Rei was born.

  Her voice is entirely synthetic, the noises made for her voicebank created using an additive process in Audicity, wich drew in a fair share of supporters for its unique sound. Enough supporters, in fact, that after hosting another crowdfunding campaign, Missile39 raised enough money to get her an official release on the Vocaloid 5 and 6 engines, though it still has not yet been released.

  • Gimmick Girl
    A song about what consiousness might mean to a machine
  • Adachi Rei
    A song about the wonderful Adachi Rei

Utane Uta 'Defoko'

  Utane Uta, or Defoko, is the default voice that comes bundled with the Utau(and therefore OpenUtau) engine. Her voice is unique even among vocal synths for being completely artificially synthesized all the way back in 2008 through the software AquesTalk.

  She has never been the most popular, though she has a dedicated fanbase that consistently put out covers and, on occasion, original songs.

  She is more commonly reffered to as Defoko, a name that means 'default girl', as she comes packaged in with Utau.

  • Weathergirl(Cover)
    A song about a love that will never come to pass
  • Lagtrain(Cover)
    A song about dealing with paranoia

Ralsei Deltarune by Samuton9574

  Ralsei is a character from Deltarune, made by Toby Fox. This voice is fanmade, and on their youtube channel -though the original creator doesn't speak much English and I don't understand much Japanese- on comments and videos said that the voice was made from taking Ralsei's in-game text sounds and putting them through a simialer process as Adachi Rei.

  Since the character already exists, this isn't an official voice, but the idea is creative, and, in my opinion, sounds pretty good.

  • Rejected Prince(Cover)
